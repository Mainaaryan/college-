Nutrition

Stay Healthy with the Right Foods

Introduction

Nutrition is the foundation of a healthy life. It provides the essential nutrients and energy needed to carry out daily activities and maintain overall health and wellbeing.

Macronutrients

There are three main macronutrients: carbohydrates, proteins, and fats. These are the main sources of energy in our diets, and each has a unique function.

Carbohydrates

Carbohydrates provide the body with a quick source of energy and can be found in foods such as bread, pasta, fruits, and vegetables. The recommended daily allowance of carbohydrates is 130 grams.

Proteins

Proteins are essential for building and repairing muscle tissue and can be found in foods such as meat, dairy products, beans, and nuts. The recommended daily allowance of protein is 46 grams for women and 56 grams for men.

Fats

Fats are important for maintaining healthy skin, insulating the body, and supporting hormone production. They can be found in foods such as avocados, olive oil, and nuts. The recommended daily allowance of fat is 20-35% of total daily caloric intake.

Vitamins and Minerals

Vitamins and minerals are essential micronutrients that play a key role in maintaining good health. Some of the most important vitamins and minerals include vitamin C, calcium, and iron.

Vitamin C

Vitamin C helps boost the immune system, improve skin health, and enhance the absorption of iron. It can be found in foods such as oranges, strawberries, and bell peppers.

Calcium

Calcium is essential for strong bones and teeth, and can be found in dairy products, leafy greens, and fortified foods. The recommended daily allowance of calcium is 1000-1300mg.

Iron

Iron helps transport oxygen in the blood and can be found in foods such as red meat, poultry, and beans. The recommended daily allowance of iron is 8mg for women and 18mg for men.
